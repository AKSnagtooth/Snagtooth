# Snagtooth — Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

## Primary ICP: The Founder-Operator

**Name:** Alex (composite)
**Role:** Founder or Co-founder
**Company Revenue:** $3M–$10M/year
**Industry:** DTC e-commerce, lifestyle, outdoor, action sports
**Team Size:** 3–15 people (lean, scrappy, no dedicated marketing department)
**Ad Spend:** $10K–$80K/month across Meta + Google

---

### Who They Are

Alex built something real. The product works. Customers love it. Word of mouth has gotten them to $3M+ — but now growth has plateaued and they know they need to buy their way into the next tier. They're not a marketing novice — they've run their own ads, hired freelancers, maybe even worked with one or two agencies. They know enough to be dangerous, and enough to know when they're being bullshitted.

Alex is competitive. Watches their CAC obsessively. Has dashboards on their phone. Gets annoyed when agencies send reports full of impressions and reach instead of cost per purchase. They've sat through enough agency "strategy calls" to have a finely tuned bullshit detector.

They value their brand deeply. The aesthetic, the voice, the cultural positioning — it matters to them. They've been burned by agencies that made generic-looking ads that felt like they could've been for any brand. They want creative that actually looks like them.

---

### Psychographics

- **Driven by results, not optics.** They don't care about awards. They care about CAC, ROAS, and monthly revenue.
- **Skeptical of agencies by default.** Has been burned before. Will Google-stalk any agency they're considering. Checks case studies for real numbers.
- **Time-starved.** Wearing multiple hats. Doesn't want to manage an agency — wants an agency that doesn't need to be managed.
- **Culturally invested.** Their brand is an extension of their identity. They want an agency that gets the vibe without a 40-page brief.
- **Respects craft.** They know good creative when they see it. A moodboard from Canva doesn't impress them.
- **Values directness.** Say what you mean. Show what you've done. Don't waste their time with decks that say nothing.

---

### Pain Language (verbatim from their internal monologue)

*"Our agency makes beautiful ads that don't convert."*
*"I have no idea what they're actually doing — I just get a report at the end of the month."*
*"We're paying $12K/month and I feel like we're one of 40 clients they don't think about."*
*"Their media buyer and creative team clearly don't talk to each other."*
*"I could probably run these ads myself better."*
*"I need someone who actually gives a shit about our numbers."*
*"We need more creative. We're running the same three ads and they're fatiguing."*
*"I want to work with people who actually know our space."*

---

### Buying Triggers

- Current agency missed targets for 2+ months in a row
- Just fired their last agency or freelancer
- Hitting a revenue plateau despite increasing ad spend
- ROAS declining and creative is stale
- New product launch coming and they need a real campaign
- Referral from a founder friend ("you should talk to Snagtooth")
- Saw a Snagtooth ad or case study and recognized the aesthetic matches their brand
- Preparing for a fundraise or acquisition and need to show a working paid acquisition engine

### Secondary ICP: The Growth-Stage CMO

**Role:** VP Marketing or CMO (hired in, not the founder)
**Dynamic:** Has to show results to the founder or board. Needs a partner who makes them look smart, delivers on time, and doesn't require hand-holding.
**Key difference from primary ICP:** More process-oriented. Wants clear SOPs, reporting cadence, and deliverable timelines. Slightly less brand-emotional than the founder. Will advocate for Snagtooth internally if the numbers are there.

---

### Who Snagtooth Is NOT For

- Pre-revenue or early-stage brands (no budget, too much education required)
- Brands under $500K/year (can't support a meaningful ad budget)
- Enterprise brands ($20M+) who need a 20-person agency with legal review
- Brands that want organic social, community management, or influencer management
- Founders who want to micromanage every creative decision
- Brands in regulated industries (healthcare, finance, legal) that add approval friction
- Anyone who needs a 60-page strategy deck before they'll spend a dollar

---

### Where They Discover Snagtooth

1. **Referral** — most likely first touch. Founder friends, agency network
2. **Meta/Instagram ads** — Snagtooth's own paid traffic
3. **Cold outreach** — DMs or emails with case study lead
4. **Googling "boutique Meta ads agency" or "DTC creative agency"**
5. **Seeing Snagtooth's client work in the wild** (recognizing the production quality)

---

### What Converts Them

In order of impact:

1. **Specific numbers from recognizable case studies** — 119x ROAS, $206 CAC on a $6,300 product, 218 enrollments vs. 63 the year before. These are conversation-enders.
2. **The integrated pitch** — "We make the ads AND run the ads. One team. No handoff." They've felt the pain of this problem and immediately get the value.
3. **The aesthetic** — The site looks like it could be one of their brand clients. Not like every other agency.
4. **No long-term contract** — Month-to-month removes the biggest risk objection.
5. **Founder credibility** — LA Kings Creative Director + performance marketing background signals rare dual expertise.
6. **Low friction to start** — Short qualifying form, fast call, clear next step.
