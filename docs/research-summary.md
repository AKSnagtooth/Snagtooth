# Snagtooth — Research Summary

## Market Position

The DTC performance marketing agency space is saturated with two failure modes: large full-service shops ($15K–$100K+/month) that are too slow, too layered, and too brand-agnostic — and cheap performance-only shops that run ads but can't make creative. The gap is wide open for a lean, creative-first + media-buying team that operates at boutique scale ($2K–$6K/month) with enterprise-quality output. Snagtooth owns that gap.

## Target Customer Profile

**Who:** Founder or CMO of a DTC or lifestyle/outdoor brand doing $3M–$10M/year. Likely has tried agencies before and got burned. Smart, moves fast, watches their numbers daily. Knows their product is good — frustrated that their ads don't reflect it. Has budget but not unlimited patience.

**What they need:** A team that gets their brand on day one, makes creative that doesn't look like every other DTC ad, and runs media that actually moves the needle — without requiring a 12-person Slack channel to get a decision made.

**What they're frustrated by:**
- Agencies that promise the world, deliver a mood board, and ghost after month 3
- Creative team and media buyer who have never spoken to each other
- Paying $15K/month for a dedicated account manager who doesn't run ads
- Vanity metrics in monthly reports (reach, impressions, engagement) instead of CAC and ROAS
- Long-term contracts that lock them in before results are proven
- Agencies that need 60 days to "ramp up" before touching the account

## Competitive Landscape

| Competitor | Positioning | Price Range | Gap |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Common Thread Collective** | Ecom growth system for $10M–$100M brands | $15K–$350K+ | Too big, too expensive, too process-heavy for $3–10M brands |
| **Kulin** | Creative + paid media for established DTC | $8K–$20K/month | Similar offering but higher price, less edge, corporate tone |
| **Hawke Media** | A la carte growth agency | $500–$5K/month | Low-touch, media-first, weak creative, cookie-cutter |
| **In-house hire** | "We'll just do it ourselves" | $6K–$15K/month (salary) | One person can't do both creative and media at this level |
| **Freelance stack** | Separate creative freelancer + media buyer | $3K–$8K/month | No integration, broken telephone, inconsistent output |

**The universal competitor complaint:** Too slow. Too expensive. Great at promises. Bad at creative that converts. The media buyer never talks to the creative team.

## Positioning Opportunity

The integrated creative + performance shop at the $3M–$10M brand tier — where the person who makes the ad is the same person (or tight two-person team) reading the data. No handoff. No layers. No 90-day ramp. Creative built to convert, not to win awards.

Secondary positioning angle: Founder credibility. Former LA Kings Creative Director isn't a footnote — it's proof that elite creative production is baked in, not outsourced.

## Pricing Context

- **Market low end:** $500–$2K/month (Hawke-style, low-touch, plug-and-play)
- **Market mid-range:** $3K–$8K/month (boutique shops, Snagtooth's zone)
- **Market high end:** $15K–$100K+/month (CTC, Power Digital, Avenue Z)
- **Snagtooth sweet spot:** $2K–$6K/month retainer — positioned as "enterprise creative quality without the enterprise invoice"
- **Proof of value anchor:** BOSS case study ($206 CAC on a $6,300 product). FA x Converse ($7.95 cost per purchase, 28.78x ROAS). These numbers end conversations.

## Key Insights

1. **The trust gap is the real sales problem.** DTC founders have been burned by agencies that overpromised. The Snagtooth website needs to lead with proof, not promises. Numbers kill skepticism faster than anything else.

2. **"Full-service" is a red flag to sophisticated buyers.** Brands at $3–10M know they don't need everything. They need two things done exceptionally: creative and paid media. Snagtooth's tight scope is a feature, not a limitation.

3. **The creative/media split is a known failure mode.** Everyone in the industry has felt the broken telephone between creative teams and media buyers. "One roof, one team, no handoff" is a message that lands immediately with anyone who's lived it.

4. **Competitor sites all sound the same.** "Data-driven." "Full-funnel." "Growth partners." It's a sea of jargon. Snagtooth's direct, specific, Western-edged voice will stand out on aesthetic alone — before anyone reads a word.

5. **The Calendly-first flow risks losing warm leads.** The form-first approach (qualifying before booking) is correct given the target client's budget expectations. Reduces noise from underqualified leads. But the form must be frictionless — name, brand, URL, monthly ad budget, biggest challenge. That's it.
