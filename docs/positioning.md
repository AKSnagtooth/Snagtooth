# Snagtooth — Positioning Angles

## Schwartz Awareness Level Assessment

**Primary traffic:** Mix of cold paid (Meta ads), warm referrals, and outbound prospects.

- Cold paid traffic → **Level 2 (Problem-Aware):** They know their ads aren't working / their agency isn't performing. They don't know Snagtooth.
- Warm referrals → **Level 3–4 (Solution-Aware / Product-Aware):** They know creative + media agencies exist. They've heard the name. They're evaluating.
- Outbound prospects → **Level 3 (Solution-Aware):** They know options exist, haven't considered Snagtooth specifically.

**Calibration:** Lead with the problem (Level 2 hook), pivot quickly to differentiation (Level 3), close with proof (Level 4). The site serves all three simultaneously.

---

## Positioning Angles (Ranked)

### 🥇 Primary Angle: The Specialist (Anti-Agency)
*"We make ads and run ads. That's it. Under one roof."*

**Why it wins:**
- Directly addresses the #1 complaint: disconnected creative and media teams
- "That's it" signals confidence and specificity — not trying to be everything
- Immediately differentiates from full-service bloat
- Resonates at every awareness level

**Core message:** The person who designs your ad is the person reading your dashboard. No handoff. No broken telephone. No wasted budget on creative that doesn't talk to your data.

---

### 🥈 Secondary Angle: The Proof
*"119x ROAS. $206 CAC on a $6,300 product. 90K followers at $0.41."*

**Why it wins:**
- Numbers are conversation-enders for skeptical DTC founders
- Specific figures signal real experience, not hypotheticals
- Works as a hook for cold paid traffic
- BOSS case study is a particularly strong proof of transformation (not just performance, but brand revival)

**Core message:** We don't sell vibes. Every engagement has a number attached to it. Here's what we've done and exactly what it produced.

---

### 🥉 Tertiary Angle: The Insider (Founder Credibility)
*"Former LA Kings Creative Director. Same creative quality. Half the overhead."*

**Why it works as support:**
- Establishes elite creative credentials immediately
- "LA Kings" is recognizable — signals major-league production quality
- Creates a "how does a brand like ours get this level of talent?" moment
- Best used in subheadlines and the "Why Us" section, not as the primary hook

---

## Angles NOT Selected (and why)

| Angle | Why Skipped |
|---|---|
| **The Speed** ("New creative this week, not next quarter") | True, but not the primary pain point for this ICP. Secondary benefit, not the lead. |
| **The Price Flip** ("$20K agency quality at $4K pricing") | Risks anchoring too early on price. Better to earn value first, then reveal pricing. |
| **The Risk Reversal** ("Month-to-month, no long-term contracts") | True and important — but works better as an objection handler than a primary angle. Include in FAQ + pricing section. |
| **The Founder Story** | Compelling but self-focused. The ICP cares about their results, not Snagtooth's origin story. Use sparingly in "Why Us" section. |

---

## Anti-Positioning Statement

*"We're not a full-service agency. We don't do organic social, SEO, influencer outreach, or community management. We do two things: creative production and paid media. If you need everything, hire someone else. If you need these two things done exceptionally well, we're the call to make."*

---

## One-Sentence Positioning Statement

**"Snagtooth is the creative production + paid media team for DTC and lifestyle brands that are done paying agencies to make ads that don't convert."**

---

## Hero Headline Options (5 Tested)

1. **"We Make the Ads. We Run the Ads. Your CAC Drops."** *(Direct, three beats, result-forward)*
2. **"Your Agency Makes Pretty Ads. We Make Ads That Sell."** *(Comparative, confrontational, memorable)*
3. **"Creative Production + Paid Media. One Team. One Roof. No Excuses."** *(Structural, differentiator-first)*
4. **"Stop Paying for Creative That Doesn't Convert."** *(Problem-first, Level 2 awareness hook)*
5. **"The Last Agency You'll Hire for Creative and Media."** *(Bold, confident, slightly dangerous)*

**Selected:** Option 2 as primary. Option 1 as subheadline. Option 4 as problem section opener.

Rationale: Option 2 stops the scroll, creates a comparison frame instantly, and lands on "sell" — the outcome the ICP actually cares about. Option 1 works as a tight follow-up that delivers the mechanism.
