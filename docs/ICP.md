# ICP.md — Ideal Customer Profile
## For Claude Code: How to Use This File

This is not a persona for a pitch deck. This is a decision filter. Before you write a word, change a headline, adjust a layout, or modify a CTA — run it through this profile. If the change wouldn't resonate with this person, revert it.

---

## The Person You're Designing For

**Name:** Alex (composite — could be male or female)
**Role:** Founder, Co-founder, or CMO
**Company:** DTC or lifestyle/outdoor brand
**Revenue:** $3M–$10M/year
**Team size:** 3–15 people
**Monthly ad spend:** $10K–$80K across Meta + Google
**Device they'll likely see this on first:** iPhone, Instagram feed, tapped a Snagtooth ad

---

## What Alex Looks Like in the Wild

Alex built something real. The product works. Word of mouth got them to $3M+ but growth has plateaued. They're not a marketing novice — they've run their own ads, hired freelancers, worked with one or two agencies. They know enough to be dangerous.

They watch their CAC obsessively. They have dashboards on their phone. They've sat through enough agency "strategy calls" to have a finely tuned bullshit detector. They value their brand deeply — the aesthetic, the voice, the cultural positioning. They've been burned by agencies that made generic ads that could've been for any brand.

**They are not:**
- Patient with vague promises
- Impressed by decks
- Looking for a 50-person agency
- Interested in organic social, SEO, or influencer management
- Willing to wait 90 days to see results
- Going to read 800 words of body copy before they know what you do

---

## What They Need to Know in the First 5 Seconds

1. **What Snagtooth does** — creative + paid media, one team, one roof
2. **Who it's for** — brands like theirs
3. **That it works** — a number, not a promise

If the hero section doesn't deliver all three, it's failing this person.

## Their Internal Monologue (Verbatim)

Use these phrases as copy benchmarks. If the site's language sounds like a response to these thoughts, it's working.

> *"Our agency makes beautiful ads that don't convert."*
> *"I have no idea what they're actually doing — I just get a report at the end of the month."*
> *"We're paying $12K/month and I feel like one of 40 clients they never think about."*
> *"Their media buyer and creative team clearly don't talk to each other."*
> *"I could probably run these ads better myself."*
> *"I need someone who actually gives a shit about our numbers."*
> *"We need more creative. We're running the same three ads and they're fatiguing."*
> *"I want to work with people who actually know our space."*

## What Converts Them — In Order of Impact

| # | Conversion Driver | Where It Lives on the Site |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Specific numbers from real case studies** | Case studies section — 119x ROAS, $206 CAC, $0.41/follow. These end conversations. |
| 2 | **The integrated pitch** | Solution section — "one team, one roof, no handoff." They've felt this pain. They get it instantly. |
| 3 | **The aesthetic** | Every section — if the site looks like it could be one of their client brands, trust goes up. |
| 4 | **No long-term contract** | Pricing section — month-to-month removes the biggest risk objection. Must be visible before CTA. |
| 5 | **Founder credibility** | Why Us / Solution — LA Kings Creative Director signals rare dual expertise. |
| 6 | **Low friction to start** | Contact form — short, two steps, fast. Any extra field costs conversions. |

## Their Objections — and Where the Site Handles Them

| Objection | How They Say It | Where It's Handled |
|---|---|---|
| **Price** | "Can't justify $4K/month right now" | Pricing section — "retainers start at $2K" anchors low |
| **Trust** | "We've been burned before" | Case studies — real numbers, real client names kill this |
| **Fit** | "Do you know our space?" | Partners bar — Vans, Adidas, Converse, BOSS signals yes |
| **Control** | "We don't want to just hand things over" | Process section — audit first, they stay informed |
| **Lock-in** | "No long contracts" | Pricing section — month-to-month, stated explicitly |
| **Capacity** | "Are we one of 50 clients?" | Pricing note — "limited new clients per quarter" |

**Every objection must be handled before the contact form.** If Alex reaches the form with an unresolved objection, they don't fill it out.

## Design Decisions This Profile Drives

### Typography and scale
Alex is looking at this on a phone, likely after seeing an ad. **The headline needs to be readable at arm's length in under 2 seconds.** If the hero type is small enough to be "refined," it's too small. Bigger is always right.

### Color and energy
Alex's brand has a point of view. They want an agency with one too. The hard-cut orange/dark color blocking signals confidence and cultural fluency — not a generic "professional services" site. **If it could work for a consulting firm, it's wrong for Snagtooth.**

### Copy density
Alex is time-starved. They skim. **Lead every section with the payoff, not the setup.** Short sentences. Specific numbers. Cut everything that doesn't earn its place. If a paragraph is making a point that's already been made, delete it.

### Social proof placement
Alex is skeptical by default. **Don't make them scroll to find proof.** Case study numbers should appear early and often. The partners bar (Vans, Adidas, Converse) belongs above the fold or immediately below it.

### CTA copy
Alex responds to direct action verbs. **"Get Started" and "Book a Call" work. "Learn More" and "Explore Our Services" don't.** Every CTA should tell them exactly what happens next.

### Form design
Alex has filled out agency forms that turned into a 45-minute sales call before they knew pricing. **The form must feel like a quick qualifying conversation, not a commitment.** Two steps, five total fields, done. The Calendly link on the confirmation is the release valve — it gives them control over when the next step happens.

### Mobile layout
Alex is on a phone. **Every section must work at 375px width with one thumb.** CTA buttons must be thumb-reachable. Text must not require pinching. Cards must stack cleanly. Test mobile before declaring anything done.

## Who This Site Is NOT For

If someone fits these descriptions, the site should not try to convert them. The anti-ICP is as important as the ICP.

- Pre-revenue / idea-stage brands (no budget)
- Brands under $500K/year (can't support meaningful ad spend)
- Anyone who needs hand-holding on basic strategy
- Founders who want to micromanage every creative decision
- Brands that primarily need organic social, SEO, or influencer work
- Anyone looking for a $500/month "managed ads" solution
- Enterprise brands ($20M+) expecting an agency with legal review and 15 stakeholders

**The pricing section and anti-positioning copy are designed to self-select these people out.** Don't soften that language. It's intentional.

## Quick Checklist — Run Before Any Change

- [ ] Does this change help Alex understand what Snagtooth does faster?
- [ ] Does this change make the proof more visible or more credible?
- [ ] Does this change reduce friction on the path to the contact form?
- [ ] Does this change handle an objection that Alex would have?
- [ ] Does this change look like it belongs on a brand Alex would respect?
- [ ] Does this change work on a 375px mobile screen?

If none of those boxes are checked, the change probably doesn't need to happen.
